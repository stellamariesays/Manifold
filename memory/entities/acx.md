# Entity: ACX (Across Protocol Token)

**Type:** DeFi protocol token / governance situation
**Direction:** inflow (active research + advocacy)
**First contact:** 2026-03-13

---

## What It Is

ACX is the governance token for Across Protocol — a cross-chain bridge that has processed ~$35B in volume. The protocol is operated/controlled by Risk Labs.

---

## The Situation

Risk Labs is proposing a buyout/restructuring that:
- Creates an SPV (Special Purpose Vehicle) with a **5M ACX minimum** to participate (US accredited investors only)
- Forces small holders into a **cash buyout at ~$0.04375** (30-day average pre-announcement)
- Current spot: ~$0.063
- All-time high: ~$1.69 (Nov 2024)
- Implied haircut for small holders: **~97% from ATH, ~30% below current spot**

**The structural problem:** Large holders who qualify for the SPV get equity upside in the ongoing business. Small holders get the cash exit at a bad price and no path to participate in future upside. The people getting the bad deal don't have the votes to stop it.

## Strategy Notes

**Leverage points:**
1. Risk Labs wants a *clean* governance approval — making the process publicly ugly creates negotiating pressure
2. The narrative "large holders vote to expropriate retail" is reputationally damaging to a company being built
3. Specific counter-ask: open SPV to all holders proportionally, no minimum, no accreditation requirement
4. If they say no publicly, they've admitted the structure is designed to exclude retail — that's the headline

**Tactical options:**
- Publish analysis on Across governance forum + crypto Twitter + Discord
- Signal-vote or shared doc to aggregate small holder "no" opposition visibly
- Crypto media pitch (The Block, Unchained, Decrypt) — story writes itself
- Legal angle: SPV structure + DAO never receiving protocol revenue = possible securities issue worth flagging informally
- Find large holders who are *also* disadvantaged (non-US, non-accredited) — they change the voting math

**What doesn't work:** Trying to buy governance power with ~$600. The play is information, coordination, and reputational pressure.
